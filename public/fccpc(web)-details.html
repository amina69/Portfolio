About The Project

The project is dedicated to developing admin, consumer & respondent web applications for the Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission (FCCPC) in Nigeria (while working for a Tech Solutions Company).

Its primary objective is to enhance the efficiency and fairness of the consumer complaints process, ensure accessible pathways for all citizens to obtain safe products, resolutions between businesses and consumers and safeguard the rights of consumers across the country.

Problem Statement
The Federal Competition and Consumer Protection Commission (FCCPC) in Nigeria faces significant challenges in efficiently managing and addressing consumer complaints, monitoring compliance, and fostering transparency in competition and consumer protection. The lack of an integrated digital platform hinders administrative efficiency, limits consumer access to resources, and complicates data collection and analysis for respondent engagement. This creates bottlenecks in ensuring fair practices, timely resolutions, and informed decision-making for stakeholders.
Research Methods
Stakeholder Interviews: Engaged with FCCPC officials, businesses, and consumers to understand pain points, priorities, and expectations.
User Surveys: Conducted surveys targeting diverse demographics to identify barriers to using existing complaint resolution systems.
Prototyping and Usability Testing: Developed and tested prototypes with end-users to refine features and user experience.
Findings
Manual processes lead to delays in complaint resolution and compliance tracking.
Absence of a unified platform for seamless interaction among all stakeholders.
Lack of centralized data storage hinders effective reporting and decision-making.
